October arrived, spreading a damp chill over the grounds and into the castle.
Madam Pomfrey, the nurse, was kept busy by a sudden spate of colds among the staff and students.
Her Pepperup potion worked instantly, though it left the drinker smoking at the ears for several hours afterward.
Ginny Weasley, who had been looking pale, was bullied into taking some by Percy. The steam pouring from under her
vivid hair gave the impression she had spontaneously caught fire.
Harry, meanwhile, felt restless. He had so much to do, and was, it seemed, never in the right place at the right time.
He had a mountain of homework to do, he had Quidditch practice, he had to try and find out what Draco Malfoy was up to,
and he had to study for the first Apparition test, which would take place
in a little over three months' time and which he was determined to pass.
